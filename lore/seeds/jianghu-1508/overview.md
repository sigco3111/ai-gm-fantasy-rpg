## [CHUNK: event -- The Demon Resurrection]

Fifty winters have passed since the great martial war of 1458, when the alliance of righteous sects sealed Macheonbaek's Heavenly Demon body within province_heavenly_demon_palace. The seal was held by nine elders who paid with their lives. Half a century of uneasy peace followed; the orthodox sects taught their students the old songs, and the rivers ran quiet.

That quiet ended in the second moon of the year 1508. Scouts from faction_white_cloud_sword_sect returned to province_cheongpungseong with tales of broken seals, drained disciples, and an altar of black stone drinking moonlight. character_macheonbaek's laughter has been heard on the ridges above the Blood Sword Forest again, and the demon-cult's banners — once thought ashes — now flutter from peaks where no fire has burned for fifty years.

The seal's breaking is more than catastrophe. It is hinge and test. faction_wudang_sect under character_nangong_soyeong has begun the long trek down from province_wudang_mountain, debating whether the seal held at all. faction_shaolin_temple's righteous army under character_hyeneung re-forms in province_shaolin_temple. The Blood Sword Alliance in province_blood_sword_forest watches the smoke with an old greed. faction_huashan_sect sharpens its blades in province_huashan, looking for a name to make.

A single sword has no answer for this. character_seol_muyeong begins the season with little more than a stripped sect-name and the memory of a slain master. What follows will decide whether the rivers of the world run in ink or in blood.

## [CHUNK: misc -- Player Position]

character_seol_muyeong is twenty-two, a junior disciple of faction_white_cloud_sword_sect, and the last of his line. Three moons past, raiders from the cult burned the White Cloud compound to its foundation stones. His shifu fell holding the inner hall. The sect's elders have scattered; the survivors — a handful of wounded brothers and sisters — cling to a mountain outpost near province_cheongpungseong, calling it still the "main hall" though it is a stone hut with a torn banner.

Seol Muyeong practices the sect's foundational sword forms (白雲三十二劍) at the edge of his ability, with a touch of inner-cultivation at the threshold of qi-sensing. He is not a prodigy; he is a young man whose pride has been broken in fire. He carries a single-edge jian his master gave him on the night of his naming day, a thread of plain red cloth tied at the pommel, and a sealed letter he has never read.

In province_cheongpungseong he meets character_chae_seobang, the silver-tongued guildmaster of the Celestial Jade Guests. The jade guests have no flag and no doctrine, only a price list and an unmatched memory for debts. Whether the player accepts a contract, walks past, or ends up drugged in a back room is a first test of temperament.

He can also seek out the wandering disciples of faction_wudang_sect, attempt the long climb to province_shaolin_temple for counsel, or follow a rumor of his surviving shixiong into the woods of province_blood_sword_forest. Each path costs days and changes the shape of the campaign.

## [CHUNK: misc -- Major Factions]

The martial world of 1508 is a tense map of powers. Use these factions as the broad strokes the GM can refine:

- faction_white_cloud_sword_sect: Disgraced but unbroken orthodox sect clinging to a hill outpost. Player-aligned, desperate, and resource-poor. The sect's pride is its sword forms and a promise to rebuild the compound.
- faction_heavenly_demon_cult: Antagonist force led by character_macheonbaek from province_heavenly_demon_palace. Revival of blood-techniques, hunger-driven, expansionist. Aims to crack the orthodox alliance before it reforms.
- faction_wudang_sect: Daoist monastic order under character_nangong_soyeong. The most senior surviving voice among the orthodox. Walks softly, strikes last, and remembers the names of every elder who died at the seal.
- faction_shaolin_temple: Buddhist monastery under character_hyeneung. Righteous army tradition; its monks fight with a serenity that unsettles younger swordsmen. The closest the orthodox have to a true army.
- faction_huashan_sect: Sword-palace sect, prestige-hungry, and quick to pick a side that wins. Trains the famous "Huashan Thirty-Six Palms" and judges everyone by their footwork.
- faction_blood_sword_alliance: Confederacy of bandit-lords and renegades operating from province_blood_sword_forest. Neither orthodox nor demonic — only hungry. The wild card the GM can use to complicate any plan.
- faction_celestial_jade_guests: Assassin-broker guild led by character_chae_seobang, headquartered in a back room of a Cheongpungseong teahouse. Information is their true weapon, and they sell it to anyone with coin or a clean-enough grudge.

Each faction name above maps to a seed entity id so the narrative remains connected to mechanical hooks and relationships.

## [CHUNK: misc -- The World at a Glance]

The year is 1508 of the Dawoon (大雲) calendar, late spring. The empire's banner still flutters over walled cities, but in the rivers and on the roads, the orthodox sects are the law most people obey. Cities like province_cheongpungseong keep watch-fires lit at night; mountain halls like province_wudang_mountain train in near-silence; bandits shelter in province_blood_sword_forest and pretend they are not afraid.

Inner cultivation (내공) is a real and rare force. A handful of grandmasters can split a stone with a palm, sense an enemy at twenty paces, or walk a snowy ridge barefoot without leaving a print. Most martial artists, including the player, are nowhere near that level — they are young men and women with steady hands, decent breath-work, and a long road ahead. The gap between a disciple and a grandmaster is not just skill; it is years of patient, often painful, practice.

The breaking of the demon seal forces a realignment. faction_heavenly_demon_cult gathers cultists in province_heavenly_demon_palace, faction_wudang_sect begins a diplomatic circuit, faction_shaolin_temple dispatches a righteous-army column, and the player's scattered shifu-brothers make their way back toward the ruin. character_nangong_soyeong's letters and character_hyeneung's sermons are lines the GM can pull. Each rumor, each step on a road, each evening spent at a fire is a turn the world turns.

Tone: atmospheric and tense, a late-spring world that smells of pine resin, mud, and old incense. Hope remains, but every night without news is a night the seal could be cracking wider.

## [CHUNK: misc -- GM Latitude]

This seed is a starting kit, not a script. The GM should treat key names as anchors, not constraints. character_macheonbaek may be revealed as a puppet of something older; the "demon" seal may have been a misunderstanding. character_seol_muyeong can shift allegiances: a player who begins with the white cloud remnant may find themselves outlawed, exalted, or something stranger.

Feel free to alter motives: faction_heavenly_demon_cult's raids could be punishment, reclamation, or a test from a higher authority. province_blood_sword_forest might secretly broker deals with cult emissaries. faction_huashan_sect could be infiltrated by a faction the player trusts. The seed includes direct hooks to the entity ids used elsewhere: faction_white_cloud_sword_sect, faction_heavenly_demon_cult, faction_wudang_sect, faction_shaolin_temple, faction_huashan_sect, faction_blood_sword_alliance, faction_celestial_jade_guests, character_seol_muyeong, character_macheonbaek, character_nangong_soyeong, character_hyeneung, character_chae_seobang, province_cheongpungseong, province_wudang_mountain, province_shaolin_temple, province_huashan, province_blood_sword_forest, province_heavenly_demon_palace.

Use the above as a scaffold. The GM may expand any faction into rival houses, create subplots in the jade-guest teahouse, or make the "demon" sympathetic. Leave room for improvisation; the world must breathe as players push into it.
